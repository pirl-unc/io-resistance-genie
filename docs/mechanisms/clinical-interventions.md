# Clinical interventions and surprising outcomes

This page tracks interventional studies with outcomes that materially shift the landscape — either because they establish new benefit in a previously ICI-refractory setting, or because they reveal resistance biology through a response or non-response surprise.

## ATOMIC: adjuvant atezolizumab + mFOLFOX6 in stage III dMMR colon cancer (NEJM phase 3)

The randomized phase 3 ATOMIC trial (NCT02912559) tested **atezolizumab (anti-PD-L1) + mFOLFOX6 for 6 months, then atezolizumab monotherapy to 12 months total** vs. mFOLFOX6 alone in 712 patients with resected stage III mismatch repair-deficient (dMMR) colon cancer[^pmid:41880612].

- **3-year DFS**: 86.3% (95% CI 81.8–89.8) vs 76.2% (95% CI 70.9–80.6)
- **HR 0.50 (95% CI 0.35–0.73), p<0.001**
- Grade ≥3 adverse events: 84.1% vs 71.9%
- Median follow-up: 40.9 months
- Overall survival: not yet mature

**Why this matters.** dMMR is the long-standing biomarker for ICI benefit in colorectal cancer, but until now the evidence was in metastatic disease. ATOMIC extends benefit to the **adjuvant stage III setting**, cutting recurrence/death events in half. Clinically this is a practice-shifting result. For this synthesis, it also shifts the locus where future resistance biology will be defined — a large new population of dMMR patients will now be on prolonged ICI exposure, creating a new substrate for characterizing late / adjuvant-setting resistance mechanisms.

**Caveats.** OS data pending. Toxicity cost is real. The benefit is specific to stage III dMMR — does not generalize to MMR-proficient disease.

## 8-gene k-TSP + mucinous histology stratifies who benefits from adding anti-CTLA-4 in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC

A composite biomarker combining an **8-gene (4 k-TSP pair) classifier with mucinous histology** identifies a 15.3% subgroup of dMMR/MSI-H metastatic CRC patients in whom adding anti-CTLA-4 to anti-PD-1 produces much larger benefit than anti-PD-1-based monotherapy[^pmid:41950572].

- Pooled analysis: n=163 (training cohort n=103 + validation n=87)
- In the biomarker-positive subgroup (Cluster A + mucinous, n=25): **24-month PFS 72.2% with combination vs. 13.8% with monotherapy; HR 0.10 (95% CI 0.02–0.39, p<0.001)**
- k-TSP balanced accuracy 68.1%
- Treatment interaction LR p ≈ 0.04

**Why this matters.** The clinical question "should all dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients get ipi + nivo, or is anti-PD-1 alone enough?" has been open for the better part of a decade. Retrospective comparisons have suggested combination benefit but at substantial toxicity cost. A biomarker that identifies the 15% where combination benefit is large (HR 0.10) would resolve this question for that subgroup.

**Caveats.** Retrospective analysis across non-randomized comparisons of different regimens. The benefiting subgroup is small (n=25). Excluded patients on anti-PD-L1 and ECOG ≥2. **Prospective validation is explicitly called for.**

## LOAd703 + atezolizumab in anti-PD-1-refractory melanoma

Covered in detail on the [TME exclusion page](tme-exclusion.md): intratumoral LOAd703 (a TMZ-CD40L/4-1BBL-armed oncolytic adenovirus) + IV atezolizumab reprogrammed the myeloid compartment toward APC-like phenotypes and restored ICI-responsive immune signatures in 24 patients with stage IV ICI-refractory melanoma[^pmid:41888981]. Few interventions reliably re-sensitize confirmed anti-PD-1-refractory melanoma to checkpoint blockade; this is a biomarker-level signal in a hard population.

## What's not in this seed

Neoadjuvant combination IO trials outside cSCC and melanoma, CAR-T + ICI combinations, bispecific T-cell engagers (with the exception of tarlatamab in SCLC, which surfaced but was not among the final extraction set), and late-line checkpoint sequencing did not surface in the top-ranked set from this three-month window.
